Chapter One: The Boy Who Remembers Too Much

This is the story of a boy who sees things that are not meant to be seen— a miracle, a curse… or something else.

Eryndor, a world full of mysteries. It was a vast world, divided into five great countries, one at the center and four surrounding it. Some were filled with wealth and light, while others were broken and drowned in darkness. Some were lost in war and silence… and some were simply forgotten.

Beyond these five great nations, there were many smaller places scattered across the map. In one forgotten corner, there was a small town—so far from any nation that most people didn’t even know it existed.

It stood between empty roads and dry lands. No trade passed through it, no travelers stayed. Only one man visited every week, bringing supplies and trading for whatever little the townspeople could gather.

There was a boy in that town. His parents had died in an accident, and he blamed himself for it. People saw him every day, but they never truly noticed him. He walked quietly, stayed out of the way, and never caused trouble.

His clothes were worn, his hands always empty—but his eyes… his eyes were always watching.

His name was Lucien Crowe. He had no home, no place to belong. He slept wherever he could—under broken roofs, beside closed shops, or sometimes just against a cold wall until morning arrived.

Once, his eyes held light… but that brightness faded the day his parents died. Since then, something inside him had changed.

Lucien didn’t care much for people, yet somehow, two connections remained. He never called them friends… but they stayed with him.

Garen, an old man who ran a tea stall, never asked questions. Whenever Lucien passed, a cup of tea would silently appear. Lucien would drink it, slowly, quietly… and leave.

Then there was Mira. She lived at the edge of the street, where houses leaned like they might collapse any moment. She talked more than anyone, always walking beside Lucien when she saw him.

“You don’t talk, do you?” she once said. He stayed silent. She smiled faintly and replied, “I think you do… maybe you just don’t talk to people.” Then she walked away.

Lucien remembered everything. Not just important things—but small, meaningless details others ignored. The sound of footsteps, the flicker in someone’s eyes when they lied, the cracks in walls he had seen once and never again.

He often wondered—why him? Why couldn’t he forget? Why did it never stop?

At first, it helped him. But slowly, his gift twisted into something else… something heavier. A curse he couldn’t control.

That evening, the town felt wrong. The sky darker than usual, the wind unnaturally still. Even silence felt louder than before.

Lucien walked through the empty streets, observing everything. He stopped in a narrow alley between two shops and sat down against the wall. A piece of bread lay nearby—he picked it up, brushing the dirt away.

Then his eyes froze. A stain on the ground. Old. Dark. Faded. As he stared, his chest tightened and the air grew heavy.

Footsteps echoed. A man stumbled into the alley, injured and bleeding. Lucien’s heart raced. “I’m not there… so why does it feel like I am…?”

Another figure appeared. Walking slowly. Calmly. The injured man couldn’t move. Then came a voice—low and cold… “Draven doesn’t forget.”

Everything vanished. Lucien blinked—the alley was empty again. The bread slipped from his hands as they trembled uncontrollably.

“What… was that…?” he whispered, but no answer came.

That night, Lucien climbed to the edge of the town walls and sat there, staring into the darkness beyond. The sky was clear, the moon silent above him—but his mind refused to rest.

“Draven doesn’t forget…” the words echoed. “Why does it feel like it knows me…?”

He closed his eyes—and saw it. A city. Vast, broken, burning. People running. Flames swallowing everything.

Lucien’s eyes snapped open. “I’ve never seen this…” he said, but even he didn’t believe it.

Far away from that forgotten town, Draven City burned. And deep within its ruins… something waited.

It had been waiting for a long time. A very long time. And somehow… it knew Lucien.
